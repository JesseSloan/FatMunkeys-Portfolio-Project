About FatMunkey

I am a dynamic figure, often seen scaling walls with my fingers and bare feet. I manage time efficiently. As a personal challenge, I tread water for for as long as I can. My record is six days. I woo women with my sensuous and godlike trombone playing, I can pilot bicycles up severe inclines with unflagging speed. I cook three-minute eggs in two minutes.

Using only a hoe and a large glass of water, I once single-handedly defended a small village in the Amazon Basin from a horde of ferocious army ants. I play bluegrass cello. When I’m bored, I build large mock-ups of famous suspension bridges in my yard. I enjoy urban hang gliding. On Wednesdays, after work, I repair electrical appliances free of charge.

Critics worldwide praise my original line of corduroy evening wear. I don’t perspire. My shaving razor never gets dull. I am not famous yet I get fan mail every week. I have been caller number nine and I have won the concert tickets several times. Last summer I toured New Jersey with a traveling centrifugal-force demonstration. I bowl 300 and I bat 400 consistently, but only when I’m alone.

I can cut carrots in half by throwing a poker card from fifty feet away with blinding speed and deadly accuracy. I know the exact location of every product on the shelves at Walmart. As a hobby, I design unbreakable cryptographic cipher systems. I never use them because I don’t believe in secrets.

On weekends, to let off steam, I participate in full-contact origami. Years ago I discovered the meaning of life but forgot to write it down. I have made extraordinary four course meals using only a cheese grater, a whisk and a toaster oven. I have played Hamlet. I recently had a conversation with Elvis.

I get eight hours of sleep in three hours. I balance, I weave, I dodge, I frolic, I caper and I prance. My bills are all paid. Sometimes, my creditors tell me I don’t need to worry about paying them back at all.
